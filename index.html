About My Zen Place

Who “My Zen Place” Is For

My Zen Place is a calming, offline sandbox designed for people who want quick, reliable relief from anxiety—especially aviophobia (fear of flying). It also speaks directly to acrophobia (fear of heights) and claustrophobia (fear of enclosed spaces), and then widens to support all anxiety types, from social and performance anxiety to those sudden, overwhelming spikes of panic. It’s built for short, restorative sessions (3–15 minutes), works fully in airplane mode, and has no ads or log-ins, so you can reach for it anywhere—gate, cabin, elevator, or train.

Why It Helps During Panic Attacks

When your nervous system flares, the fastest path back to baseline is multi-sensory grounding. My Zen Place gives you touch (sand and water), sight (gentle motion and lighting), and sound (layered ASMR) in one place—so your focus has somewhere safe to land. Open instantly, tap the water for a crisp splash, trace the sand, or start the built-in Box Breathing—each action gives immediate, controlled feedback that stabilizes attention and slows breathing.

Phobias We Target (and How the Game Supports Them)

  • Aviophobia (flight phobia): Fully offline, sized for short sessions, and designed for confined spaces. Use during boarding, takeoff, turbulence, or cruise: trace sand to steady your hands, tap the water for splash feedback, and run a ≈96‑second breathing cycle to regulate pace. An optional ambient plane fly‑by turns a trigger into a gentle point of focus.
  • Acrophobia (heights): Calm your visual field with a grounded, close camera and slow diorama motion—no jarring movement, just controllable pans that keep you centered.
  • Claustrophobia (tight spaces): Create a spacious, personal scene—open sky, water, and soft horizons—right there in an elevator, cabin, or train carriage.
  • And for all anxiety types: The tools are flexible: tactile drawing, rhythmic sound, gentle motion, structured breathing, and quick “reset” views help with generalized anxiety, social spikes, or unexpected panic.

What you do: Draw lines, circles, spirals in fine, tactile sand; dip into interactive water that ripples to your touch.

What you feel: Instant cause‑and‑effect calms racing thoughts; your hands “talk” to your attention.

Signature detail: The splash sound on every water tap—clean, soft, and precise—acts like an audio anchor. People report it as the fastest “I’m back here” moment in the app.

Layered ASMR & Relaxing Audio

What you do: Mix nature (wind, rain, birds), gentle piano, and an optional 528 Hz tone. Toggle groups on/off to build your perfect sound bed.

What you feel: A calm, low‑stimulation soundscape that reduces internal noise without overwhelming you.

Box‑Breathing Mini‑Scene (≈96 seconds)

What you do: Follow 4s inhale → 4s hold → 4s exhale → 4s hold, six times. Visual cues and optional voice prompts keep you on rhythm.

What you feel: Heart rate slows, chest eases, and the mind locks onto a simple, repeatable cadence. Perfect during turbulence or tight quarters.

Meditation Camera & Time‑Lapse

What you do: Let the camera spiral calmly around your scene while time and light glide from day to dusk to night.

What you feel: A soft “perspective shift” that interrupts worry loops and invites curiosity instead of fear.

Day–Night & Weather Cycles

What you do: Watch light change naturally; add gentle rain with a slider that also tunes raindrop SFX. At night, fireflies and soft glow effects appear.

What you feel: External rhythm replaces internal urgency—nature’s pace becomes your pace.

Travel‑Friendly Design

What you get: Fully offline, launches fast, ideal in airplane mode, built for short sessions. One‑time purchase unlocks everything; no ads to break immersion.

What you feel: Trust and safety. You own the space and can reach it anytime—no surprises.

A Quick “In‑Flight Reset” or in Any Panic Attack You Can Rely On

  1. Open My Zen Place (works without internet).
  2. Tap the water a few times—listen for the splash—and trace a line in the sand.
  3. Start Box Breathing (≈96 seconds).
  4. Let the Meditation Camera make a slow turn; add light rain if you like the sound.
  5. When you feel steadier, save your scene and return to it next time you fly.

Built‑In Comforts & Accessibility

  • Visual and audio breathing cues (use either or both).
  • Landscape orientation, clean UI, rounded edges, and soft color palettes for comfort.

My Zen Place is your personal, pocket‑sized sanctuary—ready when you board, when the seatbelt sign dings, or when anxiety knocks unexpectedly. Breathe, tap, splash, and let your senses guide you back to calm.
